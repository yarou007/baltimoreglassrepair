Emergency Board Up Cost in Baltimore: What Changes the Price?

People often ask for a price over the phone. The honest answer: board up pricing depends on scope. Below are the main variables that move the cost up or down, and how to get a fast, accurate quote.

1) Size and number of openings

One broken window opening is different from multiple storefront panels. Larger openings generally mean more material and more secure fastening.

2) Access, safety, and work conditions

  • Second-story windows vs ground level
  • Busy sidewalks where crowd control is needed
  • Weather and visibility at night

3) After-hours dispatch

Emergency response outside normal business hours can change availability and scheduling. If the site is a public risk, securing it sooner is often worth it.

4) Storefront vs residential needs

Commercial board up is often about keeping the business secure and presentable until the correct glass is ready. Residential board up is often about safety, weather protection, and peace of mind.

5) What happens next (replacement planning)

Board up is temporary. If replacement is needed, we’ll help plan the correct unit on a follow-up visit: glass replacement. If you’re deciding which comes first, read board up vs replacement.

How to get a fast quote

Call 703-244-0559 and share the address, type of opening (storefront/door/window), and whether anyone is at risk. If you can, text or email photos showing the full opening.
