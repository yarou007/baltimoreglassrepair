How to Keep a Broken Window Safe Until Emergency Glass Repair Arrives

Residential · Window glass repair

A broken home window in Baltimore rowhomes or county houses presents three risks: cuts, heat loss, and false confidence in “quick fixes.” This guide stays realistic—no promises that tape equals security.

Contain the area

Keep children and pets out of the room if possible. If you must enter, wear thick gloves and shoes; assume invisible splinters on the sill.

Remove loose pieces only when safe

Hang-on shards sometimes fall unexpectedly. If a piece wiggles, don’t tug—mark the zone and let our techs finish. For ground-floor openings facing alleys, consider board up if you can’t secure the house.

Weather and HVAC

In winter, close interior doors to isolate the room; don’t crank heat blindly if airflow paths are odd in older Charles Village or Federal Hill homes with hot-water radiators near sills.

What not to do

Avoid leaning on compromised door glass panels; don’t rely on plastic wrap as “security.” See also tape and seal limits.
