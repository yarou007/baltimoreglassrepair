Board up & replacement

What is the difference between board up and full glass replacement?

Board up secures the opening temporarily. Replacement installs the finished lite.

How long can a board up safely protect my business until glass replacement?

Temporary protection is meant for days to a short window while glass is fabricated—not as a permanent solution. Weather, tampering, and traffic affect durability.

Commercial

Can a cracked commercial glass door be repaired or must it be replaced?

Tempered door lites often require replacement once compromised. We evaluate in person—don’t assume tape is enough.

Do you need tempered or laminated glass for your business?

Depends on application and code. We confirm requirements for storefront and door openings.

Commercial property emergency checklist

  • Restrict access to broken glass zones; post staff at customer approaches if needed
  • Coordinate with security or law enforcement when a crime scene is active
  • Photograph damage for insurance before cleanup when safe and permitted
  • Call for board up if the opening can’t be secured
  • Notify tenants or franchise partners per your lease playbook
  • Schedule replacement with accurate measurements

Homeowner broken glass safety checklist

  • Keep people and pets away from shards; wear thick gloves if you must handle material
  • Avoid leaning on compromised door rails
  • Contain the area; if heat or security is an issue, ask about temporary cover
  • Call 703-244-0559 for the safest next step based on your opening

Is storefront glass repair covered by insurance in Baltimore?
