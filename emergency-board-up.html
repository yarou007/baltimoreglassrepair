FAQ

When is board up the right first step?

When glass is missing or unsafe and replacement can’t happen immediately (after-hours, supplier lead times, or custom sizes), board up helps protect against weather and intrusion until replacement is ready.

How long can board up stay in place?

Boarding is temporary protection meant to bridge days—not months. Conditions, traffic, and security risks matter; we’ll tell you what’s realistic for your opening.

Do you replace the glass after board up?

Yes. If replacement is needed, we can help measure and plan the correct unit. Start here: glass replacement.

Do you handle storefronts and commercial entries?

Yes—storefront openings, entry door glass, and sidelites. For commercial-focused service, see commercial glass services.
